# Stallion — Voiceover Script (Clean Read-Aloud)

**Target runtime:** 4:30–4:50 (under 5 minutes)

---

## 0:00–0:25 — Cold Open (camera only)

If you’re a customs broker in Trinidad, you know this routine.
You get an invoice from a client. You open ASYCUDA. You start typing—consignee, consignor, HS code, invoice value, currency, exchange rate, delivery terms.
Then you calculate the duty worksheet in Excel, reformat it, generate a costing for the client, and produce the XML.
That’s 30 to 45 minutes per declaration, a hundred times a month.
**Stallion does it in under three minutes. Let me show you.**

---

## 0:25–0:50 — Step 1: Upload Documents

I have three documents from a real shipment: a commercial invoice, a FedEx air waybill, and a delivery receipt.
I drag all three into Stallion and click **Extract**.
Stallion reads all three documents together and pulls the key declaration data—consignee, invoice value, AWB number, freight charges, and more.

---

## 0:50–1:40 — Step 2: Workbench Review

Now we’re in the workbench.
Extraction has already populated the core fields—consignee, consignor, invoice value, AWB number, gross weight, and package count.
The client directory matches the importer and auto-fills TIN and consignee code.
On the right, the completion checklist shows exactly what’s complete and what still needs review.
Green is done; red is what I need to verify.
Delivery terms and freight were extracted directly from source documents.
I didn’t type any of this manually.

---

## 1:40–2:20 — Step 3: HS Code Lookup

Most invoices don’t include a clean HS code, so I use Stallion’s HS lookup.
I enter the product description and Stallion searches over 4,600 official Trinidad and Tobago tariff entries.
Results appear instantly.
I pick the correct code, and Stallion auto-populates the HS code, duty rate, and VAT rate on the line item.

---

## 2:20–2:50 — Step 4: Live Duty Calculation

The worksheet calculates in real time.
EX-WORKS value, exchange rate, freight, CIF, duty, VAT, and total payable are all chained and updated live.
If I adjust one upstream value, every downstream total recalculates immediately.
No spreadsheet errors, no broken formulas.

---

## 2:50–3:30 — Step 5: Validate and Generate

Before generating, Stallion runs a full preflight validation against declaration rules.
It checks required fields, HS formatting, value consistency, and structural readiness.
Everything passes.
I click **Generate**, and Stallion produces:
1. C82 SAD XML for ASYCUDA import,
2. Duty worksheet PDF,
3. Costing estimate for the client.
All downloadable immediately.

---

## 3:30–3:50 — Step 6: Brokerage Invoice

Now I click **Generate Invoice**.
Stallion pulls the declaration reference, consignee, and fee settings to create the brokerage invoice.
So from one upload, we now have four outputs ready.

---

## 3:50–4:15 — Step 7: Broker Review Queue

Every declaration enters a broker review queue before sign-off.
Items are prioritized by confidence so the highest-risk declarations are reviewed first.
The broker can approve, flag for correction, or reject.
Each action is logged with full audit history.
